Anatomy Basics

A Hisser's front legs have three joints: the elbow, knee, and fetlock. Their legs follow caprine anatomy. All feet traits must have this anatomy, unless it has a mutation, even traits like the 'paw' trait.


For anatomy that a cat would have, you must have the wacky limbs mutation in order to achieve this.

Mouth Anatomy

A Hoofhisser's mouth is very similar to that of a snake's. Hoofhisser's have fang sheaths, retractable fangs, forked tongues, and a glottis. The glottis is essential for eating prey because it can take up to a few hours to swallow large prey.

The tongue can be stylized to not come from a tongue sheath.

Eating Habits


Unlike regular goats, Hoofhissers can unhinge their jaws to swallow prey whole.

As obligate carnivores, Hissers will spend every few days finding a large meal to eat. They never shy away from prey even when the prey is bigger than themselves. While digesting particularly large prey, Hoofhissers move slower and are more vulnerable to attacks from other Hissers. The larger the prey, the more time a Hoofhisser has until it must eat again.

They can go for about a week in between meals.

Heat Pits

All Hoofhissers have heat pits like snakes do. These pits give them infared vision which makes hunting at night easier. They can have up to three or four heat pits.


Heat pits can be located from the nostrils to the eyes or along the mouth.

Heat pits cannot be placed on the cheek.

Skull Structure

Hoofhisser skulls differ from regular goat skulls in various ways.

The main head has a split jaw like that of a regular snake.

At the base of the horns is a tough horn that curves around the snake. It serves to protect the Hisser's snakes while they ram into things.

The snakes horns are have most of their mobility towrads their heads and they lose mobility the closer the get towards the base of the main head's skull.

Average Height

Hoofhisser's are measured at the shoulder, or "withers", like regular goats. They can range from 6'1" (73in / 185.42cm) tall to 3'8" (44in / 111.76cm).

Life Stages

A Hoofhisser will go through four main stages in its life:

Egg

They first start in an egg.

They can lay up to three eggs in one clutch.

Hissling

The Hissling stage is the most akin to real snakes.

Depending on the horn trait, this stage can have several different heads. Each head has a brain along with another brain within the "chest" area. This section will grow into its own head later in life.

Hooflet

Their middle stage of life is their most vulnerable.

Their scales shed and fur replaces it. The main head begins to form; a mouth has not formed quite yet.

As they get used to their new legs they walk very clumsily and cannot move well. Most dont survive past this stage.

Hoofhisser

Their legs and fur have fully grown in now. At their final stage they have full control of their new bodies.


The challenging feat in making it past the Hooflet stage drives them to prove their strength by fighting other Hoothissers.
